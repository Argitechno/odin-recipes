Instant Pot Yoghurt

Description

It’s surprisingly simple to turn just milk and a spoonful of yogurt into a big batch of rich, creamy Instant Pot yogurt. The process is mostly hands-off: heat the milk, cool it, whisk in a bit of active-culture yogurt, and let the Instant Pot incubate it for several hours until it thickens and gently jiggles. After a chill in the fridge, it becomes smooth, tangy, and ready to eat. This homemade version costs far less than store-bought, and you can adjust the texture however you like—strain it for Greek-style thickness or leave it as is for a milder, looser yogurt. It keeps well for over a week and even provides its own starter for future batches.

Ingredients

  • 1 gallon milk
  • 1/4 cup yogurt with active cultures (or yogurt starter)

Steps

  1. Clean or sterilize the Instant Pot
  2. Heat milk to 180–200°F
  3. Set Instant Pot to Yogurt → Adjust → BOIL/li>
  4. When it beeps, confirm milk reached 180–200°F.
  5. Cool milk to 110–115°F (naturally or in ice bath).
  6. Remove any milk skin.
  7. Mix a ladle of warm milk with yogurt starter.
  8. Whisk starter mixture back into the pot.
  9. Place pot back into Instant Pot and close lid.
  10. Set Yogurt function for 8–12 hours.
  11. Check that yogurt is set and jiggly.
  12. Refrigerate yogurt for at least 4 hours.
  13. Store in containers for up to 10 days.
